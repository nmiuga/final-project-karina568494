# reflection.md

## Overall Learnings

Overall, I learned a great deal about user interfaces. When I ran my app, I would find myself instinctively trying to use the app in certain ways and I used those instincts to improve the flow of the app. I added features to make using the app feel natural and easy. In addition, I also learned how to be more specific with my AI prompt. My initial prompt was ultimately too broad and while applying changes, I found that giving the AI a point of reference was helpful in achieving the results I wanted. For example, letting the AI know that I liked the look/layout of a page and to apply it across all pages resulted in more success for me versus trying to describe the look I was going for. 

## Challenges

Trying to make a more developed app with various functions proved to be much more difficult than any other project. Before with other projects, I could read through the code and try to customize some aspects myself without AI. However, in this app, a single file had over 1000 lines of code and it was just not going to be a productive use of my time to read through all the code. When I turned to AI to change small things that I could usually edit without AI, sometimes that resulted in errors and/or the AI changing aspects I wanted to leave the same. Throughout this process, it was necessary to pay attention to detail after each AI ask to ensure all the pages in my app still looked and functioned the way I wanted them to.

## Next Steps

I would continue this app by adding more features and improving design features. I think some colors could stand out more. Additionally, making the app more customizable would make it appeal to a wider variety of users. I also thought of the idea of adding a reward side to the app. For example, the worm in the logo could grow or be fed as the user hits their goals every day.
